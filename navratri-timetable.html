Navratri Fasting Timetable

Satvik · 9 Sacred Days · Stay Light & Energized

Your Allowed Foods: Fruits · Milk · Yogurt · Makhana · Roasted Peanuts · Sweet Potato (ghee) · Roasted Potato (ghee) · Evening meal without onion-garlic (satvik).
Goal: Light enough to stay active & focused. Never too full, never too hungry.
💧 Daily reminder: Drink 8–10 glasses of water or coconut water throughout the day. Hydration prevents fatigue during fasting.
01

Shailputri — The Beginning

Start strong · Establish the rhythm

🌸 Goddess
Shailputri
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Warm Milk + Seasonal Fruit

  • 🥛 1 glass warm milk with mishri
  • 🍌 1 banana
  • 🍎 1 apple (sliced)

Keeps you full 3–4 hrs. Banana gives potassium energy, milk gives sustained fuel.

Midday 12:30 PM Light Snack

Makhana Chaat + Yogurt

  • 🍿 1 cup roasted makhana (ghee + sendha namak)
  • 🥣 1 small bowl yogurt
  • 🍯 Drizzle of honey optional

Light protein, keeps brain sharp. Don't overeat — just enough to silence hunger.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Aloo Sabzi + Kuttu Puri (or Sweet Potato)

  • 🥔 Roasted potato sabzi in ghee
  • 🍠 Boiled sweet potato with ghee & jeera
  • 🥣 1 bowl yogurt raita

Eat moderately — 70% full. Stop before you feel heavy. No onion, no garlic.

Day 1 Tip: Begin gently. Don't skip the morning milk — it anchors your metabolism for the day.
02

Brahmacharini — Discipline

Build the habit · Stay light

🌸 Goddess
Brahmacharini
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Fruit Bowl + Yogurt

  • 🍇 Mixed fruit bowl (grapes, papaya, pomegranate)
  • 🥣 1 small bowl yogurt
  • 🌰 5–6 soaked almonds

Pomegranate seeds give iron, papaya aids digestion. Almonds give lasting energy.

Midday 12:30 PM Light Snack

Roasted Peanuts + Coconut Water

  • 🥜 Small handful roasted peanuts (sendha namak)
  • 🥥 1 glass coconut water or lemon water

Peanuts are protein powerhouse. Keeps you sharp without heaviness.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Sweet Potato Chaat + Milk

  • 🍠 Roasted sweet potato with ghee, jeera, sendha namak
  • 🍋 Squeeze of lemon + coriander leaves
  • 🥛 1 glass warm milk before bed

Sweet potato is filling and nutritious. Milk at night helps deep sleep.

Day 2 Tip: Coconut water is your best friend — it replenishes electrolytes and prevents headaches from fasting.
03

Chandraghanta — Courage

Midway through the first half · Stay consistent

🌸 Goddess
Chandraghanta
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Banana Milkshake + Makhana

  • 🥛 Banana milkshake (milk + banana, no sugar)
  • 🍿 Small cup roasted makhana

Milkshake is energizing and quick. Makhana adds crunch and protein.

Midday 1:00 PM Light Snack

Yogurt + Fruit

  • 🥣 1 bowl thick yogurt (dahi)
  • 🍎 1 apple or pear

Cooling and light. Yogurt probiotics help digestion during fasting.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Potato Sabzi + Singhare Puri

  • 🥔 Roasted potato in ghee with tomatoes & jeera
  • 🫓 2 singhare/kuttu flour puris (or skip if no flour)
  • 🥣 Yogurt on the side

Two puris is enough — don't go beyond. Stop at 70% fullness.

Day 3 Tip: By day 3, your body adjusts. If you feel headachey, increase water and add a pinch of sendha namak to lemon water.
04

Kushmanda — Energy

Halfway there · Maintain momentum

🌸 Goddess
Kushmanda
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Papaya + Warm Milk

  • 🍈 1 bowl fresh papaya
  • 🥛 1 glass warm milk with mishri or cardamom

Papaya is an excellent digestive. Light yet satisfying to start the day.

Midday 12:30 PM Light Snack

Makhana + Peanuts Mix

  • 🍿 ½ cup makhana (roasted in ghee)
  • 🥜 Small handful peanuts
  • 🧂 Sendha namak, pepper to taste

Trail mix style — satisfying, protein-rich, and keeps energy steady.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Sweet Potato + Curd Rice Style

  • 🍠 Mashed sweet potato with ghee & sendha namak
  • 🥣 1 bowl yogurt
  • 🍌 1 banana for dessert

Creamy and satisfying without heaviness. Perfect mid-fast evening meal.

Day 4 Tip: You are halfway! Banana after the evening meal gives potassium that prevents muscle cramps during the fast.
05

Skandamata — Nourishment

Refresh the menu · New energy

🌸 Goddess
Skandamata
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Mixed Fruit Plate + Yogurt

  • 🍓 Strawberries / grapes / orange slices
  • 🥣 Thick yogurt with a drizzle of honey
  • 🌰 5 soaked walnuts or almonds

Colorful fruit gives antioxidants and natural sugars for morning focus.

Midday 12:30 PM Light Snack

Roasted Peanuts + Lemon Water

  • 🥜 Roasted peanuts (1 small handful)
  • 🍋 Lemon water with sendha namak & jeera powder

Jeera lemon water is great for digestion and replenishes minerals.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Potato Sabzi + Makhana Kheer

  • 🥔 Aloo sabzi (ghee, jeera, tomato, sendha namak)
  • 🍿 Small bowl makhana kheer (milk + makhana + mishri)

Makhana kheer is a treat — makes fasting feel festive. Have a small portion.

Day 5 Tip: Makhana kheer is naturally filling. If you make it, keep the portion small — it's richer than it looks!
06

Katyayani — Strength

Three days left · Stay strong

🌸 Goddess
Katyayani
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Warm Milk + Banana

  • 🥛 1 glass warm milk (cardamom + mishri)
  • 🍌 2 bananas

Simple and powerful. Bananas are the best fasting breakfast — slow energy release.

Midday 1:00 PM Light Snack

Makhana Bowl

  • 🍿 1 cup roasted makhana (ghee + pepper + sendha namak)
  • 🥥 Coconut water or lemon water

Makhana is low calorie but filling — perfect midday snack.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Sweet Potato Tikki + Yogurt

  • 🍠 Sweet potato tikki (ghee roasted, sendha namak)
  • 🥣 Thick yogurt dip
  • 🍈 Small fruit plate as dessert

Tikkis are fun and festive. Make them small so you have 3–4 pieces max.

Day 6 Tip: Day 6 can bring fatigue. If you feel low, add 5 soaked almonds to your morning — they are a natural energy booster during fasts.
07

Kalaratri — Fearlessness

Almost there · Don't break routine

🌸 Goddess
Kalaratri
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Fruit Smoothie

  • 🥛 Milk + banana + pomegranate blended (no sugar)
  • 🌰 5–6 soaked almonds on the side

Smoothie is quick and very filling. Drink slowly — don't rush it.

Midday 12:30 PM Light Snack

Yogurt + Makhana

  • 🥣 1 bowl yogurt with honey drizzle
  • 🍿 ½ cup makhana on the side

A cooling, calming midday snack. Perfect if the day feels hot or tiring.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Aloo Sabzi + Sweet Potato

  • 🥔 Potato sabzi with ghee, jeera, tomato
  • 🍠 Half a sweet potato (boiled, with ghee)
  • 🥣 Yogurt raita

Keep portions moderate. You're near the end — maintain the light feeling.

Day 7 Tip: Day 7 is a mental challenge. Remind yourself: just 2 more days. The body has already adapted — trust it.
08

Mahagauri — Purity

Ashtami · The most sacred day

🌸 Goddess
Mahagauri
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Warm Milk + Fruits

  • 🥛 Warm milk with cardamom
  • 🍎 Apple + 🍌 banana
  • 🌹 Begin the day with aarti/puja first

Ashtami is sacred — begin with puja before eating. Keep morning very light.

Midday 1:00 PM Light Snack

Roasted Peanuts + Coconut Water

  • 🥜 Small handful peanuts
  • 🥥 Coconut water

Kanjak puja may happen today — eat after prasad rituals are done.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Puri + Chana (Prasad Style) + Halwa

  • 🫓 Kuttu/singhare puri (satvik)
  • 🍯 Small portion halwa (suji or banana)
  • 🥣 Yogurt

Ashtami evening is a celebration! Enjoy the prasad meal — just don't overeat.

Day 8 Tip: Ashtami is festive and halwa is heavy — have a very small portion. The joy is in the ritual, not the quantity.
09

Siddhidatri — Completion

Navami · Final day · Victory! 🎉

🌸 Goddess
Siddhidatri
Morning 7:30 AM Break Fast

Fruit Plate + Yogurt

  • 🍓 Full fruit plate — your favourite fruits
  • 🥣 Thick yogurt
  • 🌰 Soaked almonds + walnuts

Last day! Celebrate with a beautiful fruit plate. You've done it.

Midday 12:30 PM Light Snack

Makhana + Warm Milk

  • 🍿 Roasted makhana
  • 🥛 Warm turmeric milk (haldi doodh)

Turmeric milk on the last day gives an anti-inflammatory boost. A wonderful tradition.

Evening 6:30 PM Main Meal

Feast! (Satvik) — Break the fast with love

  • 🥔 Aloo sabzi in ghee
  • 🍠 Sweet potato
  • 🫓 Puri
  • 🍯 Halwa or makhana kheer
  • 🥣 Yogurt

You completed 9 days! Celebrate, but eat slowly and mindfully — your gut has been on a gentle diet. Don't overload it suddenly.

Day 9 Tip: After the fast ends, reintroduce regular food slowly over 1–2 days. Your digestion is cleansed — honour that by not diving straight into heavy meals.
